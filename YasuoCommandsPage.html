/create_encounter

Mainly used by the DM to set up a combat encounter. By specifying the characters (+monsters) involved, and optionally assigning their character owners (@handles). It initiates the encounter within the bot's framework, allowing for turn order, action, health and condition tracking to streamline combat.

/apply

Often used by players unwilling to use the bot to manually apply various effects to characters, such as damage, healing, or conditions. This also gives the Dungeon Master (DM) direct control over character states within the bot's system, allowing for flexible adjustments during combat or other scenarios.

/cast

This command allows a player to cast a spell. The user specifies the spell name, the target(s) of the spell, and the caster. The bot then handles all the necessary rolls and modifiers for casting that spell, simplifying the process for new players.

/attack

/attack: This command facilitates a character's attack using a specified weapon. The user inputs the attacker, the weapon, and the target, and the bot calculates if the attack hits (considering modifiers) and the damage dealt, streamlining the attack resolution process for players.

/cast (list)

When casting spells, a list of targets may be entered for spells with multible projectics and AOE. All attacks take into account the vulnerability, resistances and immunities of its target. Additionaly critical hits are automaticly taken into account for ease of use, effectively doubling the damage dice.

/secondary attack

An optional extra paramater for /attack, mainly for duelweilding and sneak attacks for rogues. Damage from attacks are applied to the target and death saves are made automatically if a character reaches zero hp. Three fails; your removed from the turn order. Three successes; your revived with 1hp.

/action

Used to perform various combat actions other than attacks. As seen in the example, it can be used for actions like "Dodge" or "Hide", abstracting the specific rules for these actions and letting the bot manage their effects on the character and combat.

/remove & /reset

The /remove command is used to manually remove a condition from a character, giving the DM control over ongoing effects. The /reset command provides a way to revert the character database to a previous backup state, which can be useful for correcting errors or resetting the combat state.
